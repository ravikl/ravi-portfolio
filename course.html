Introduction

Information is an integral extension to our biological needs for survival. The innate ability to process cues from nature (external world) through our senses to locate food or escape danger is possibly the fundamental origin to the need for information. So also, the animal and plant species to receive, process, respond and signal information for collective survival and evolution. However, development of language ( oral, verbal and visual) as code of communication has distinguished human evolution leading up to becoming what we call as information society today. From its origin as cues for survival we see information today as an economic commodity: a competitive edge to possess for success and survival. The revolution of big data enabled by the internet has transformed our lives to thrive on the fuel of information. Our imminent personal or professional actions, decisions and choices are driven by information prompts we constantly receive.
